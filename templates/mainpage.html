Chest Disease Detection

This chest disease diagnosis project aims to develop algorithms or systems that can accurately categorize chest diseases, such as pneumonia, tuberculosis, or lung cancer, based on medical imaging data like X-rays or CT scans, ultimately aiding in early diagnosis and treatment planning. These projects typically involve machine learning techniques to analyze imaging features and distinguish between different chest diseases with high accuracy and efficiency.

Abstract

Exploring the application of Deep Learning (DL) in diagnosing chest diseases. Chest diseases, such as pneumonia, tuberculosis, and lung cancer, present significant health challenges that require timely and accurate diagnosis. The model is developed using a Deep Learning (DL) based system that uses chest X-ray and clinical data to make diagnostic predictions. The performance is evaluated against conventional diagnostic methods, demonstrating the potential of Deep Learning to provide faster and more reliable chest disease diagnosis.

List of Diseases

Tuberculosis

Tuberculosis (TB) is an infectious disease that most often affects the lungs and is caused by a type of bacteria. It spreads through the air when infected people cough, sneeze or spit. Tuberculosis is preventable and curable.

Lung Cancer

  • Adenocacinoma

    • Adenocacinoma is a type of cancer that forms in the glandular tissue, which lines certain internal organs and makes and release substances in the body, such as mucus, digestive juices, and other fluids.

  • Large Cell Carcinoma Left

    • Large Cell Carcinoma Left (LCC) is categorized as a type of NSCLC (Non-Small-Cell Lung Carcinoma) which originates from epithelial cells of the lung. LCLC is histologically characterized by the presence of large, undifferentiated cells that lack distinctive features of either squamous cell carcinoma or adenocarcinoma.

  • Squamous Cell Carcinoma (SCC)

    • Squamous Cell Carcinoma (SCC) accounts for the vast majority of malignancies of the oral cavity and oropharynx. There are more than half a million incident cases of squamous-cell carcinoma of the head and neck worldwide each year, primarily affecting the oropharynx, oral cavity, hypopharynx, and larynx.

Pnuemonia

Pneumonia is an infection that inflames the air sacs in one or both lungs. The air sacs may fill with fluid or pus (purulent material), causing cough with phlegm or pus, fever, chills, and difficulty breathing.

Covid

Coronavirus disease (COVID-19) is an infectious disease caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus. Most people infected with the virus will experience mild to moderate respiratory illness and spread primarily from person to person through small droplets from the nose or mouth, expelled when a person.

Algorithm Used

CNN

CNN is a deep learning architecture that specializes in image analysis, a critical aspect of our lung disease prediction project.It is tailored for processing medical images, particularly chest X-rays, and contributes to the project in the following ways:

  • Image Feature Extraction:

    • CNN automatically extracts intricate features from X-ray images, recognizing patterns, abnormalities, and relevant visual cues. It plays a pivotal role in diagnosing heart diseases based on image data.

  • Data Fusion:

    • CNN seamlessly integrates image data with structured data, facilitating a comprehensive approach to heart disease prediction. By combining predictions from structured data models like XGBoost and AdaBoost, the ensemble model leverages the image analysis capabilities of CNN to enhance overall accuracy.
